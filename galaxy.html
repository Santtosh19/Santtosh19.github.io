Galaxy Classification Using Discriminants

Final Year Thesis | Universiti Sains Malaysia

This project explores the efficiency of various discriminants, including Artificial Neural Networks (ANN), Boosted Decision Trees (BDT), Fisher, Mahalanobis, H-Matrix, and Linear Discriminants in classifying stars and galaxies.

Different variable transformations (none, N, U, G, D, PCA) were applied to evaluate their impact on accuracy. Results showed that ANN and BDT led in performance, while Fisher and Mahalanobis excelled with the U transformation ‚Äî particularly in distinguishing rounded vs no-bulge galaxies.
